A single PTZ (Pan-Tilt-Zoom) camera can cover a parking lot, warehouse aisle, or construction site that would take 4–6 fixed bullets. But PTZ costs 3–5× more, has moving parts that fail, and only sees where it's pointed. This guide helps you decide when PTZ earns its keep—and which models deliver.

<Badge variant="outline">TL;DR</Badge> **Use PTZ for:** Large open areas (parking, yards, perimeters), active monitoring (security desk), auto-tracking intruders. **Skip PTZ for:** Hallways, small rooms, entry choke points, "set and forget" installs — fixed wide-angle or multi-sensor is cheaper and more reliable.

## PTZ vs Fixed vs Multi-Sensor: The Math

| Approach | Cameras Needed | Total Cost (Cams Only) | Coverage Gaps | Best For |
|----------|----------------|------------------------|---------------|----------|
| **4× Fixed 4K Bullets** | 4 | ~$520 | None (simultaneous) | 24/7 recording, evidence everywhere |
| **2× 180° Multi-Sensor** | 2 | ~$700 | Minimal (stitch seams) | Wide halls, building corners |
| **1× PTZ (25× Optical)** | 1 | ~$450–1,200 | **Yes — only sees where aimed** | Active monitoring, cost savings |

<Callout type="warning">
**PTZ Blind Spot Rule:** A PTZ at 25× zoom sees ~2° FOV. At 100 ft, that's a **3.5 ft wide slice**. An intruder 10 ft left of center is invisible. PTZ *requires* either a guard watching or smart auto-tracking + wide-angle secondary camera.
</Callout>

## Optical vs Digital Zoom: The Only Spec That Matters

| Zoom Type | How It Works | Image Quality at Max | Use Case |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|----------|
| **Optical** | Physical lens elements move | **Full sensor resolution** (4K/8MP at 25×) | License plates, faces, distant detail |
| **Digital** | Crop + upscale in firmware | Degrades fast (4K → 1080p → 720p) | "Nice to have" only |
| **Hybrid** | Optical + limited digital | Optical range = true quality | Marketing spec — check optical only |

**Spec to watch:** `Optical Zoom: 25×` or `30×` — ignore "300× digital zoom."  
**Lens range example:** 4.8–120 mm (25×) on 1/1.8" sensor = ~58° wide → 2.3° tele.

## Auto-Tracking AI: Does It Replace a Guard?

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="tracking-work">
    <AccordionTrigger>How Auto-Tracking Works (And Fails)</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**Typical pipeline:**
1. **Wide FOV detection** (sub-stream, 1080p/4MP) → Person/Vehicle AI
2. **Target lock** → PTZ motors slew to center target
3. **Zoom in** → Optical zoom to preset level (usually 8–15×)
4. **Follow** → Continuous pan/tilt to keep centered
5. **Return** → After dwell time (10–60s), return to "home" preset

**Where it fails:**
- **Multiple targets:** Picks largest/closest; loses others
- **Fast lateral movement:** Motors can't slew 100°/sec — target exits FOV
- **Occlusion:** Tree, truck, pillar breaks lock → returns home
- **Night IR:** AI detection range drops 50–70%; PTZ zooms into darkness
- **PTZ "hunting":** Wind vibration + sensitive detection = constant motion = motor wear

**Vendors with decent tracking (2026):** Dahua WizSense/WizMind, Hikvision AcuSense/DeepinView, Axis Q61/Q63, Hanwha XNP, Reolink RLC-823A (budget)

**Hybrid deployment (recommended):**
- **PTZ** at center/pole — auto-tracks, zooms for ID
- **2–4 Fixed wide-angle** (180° or 4K bullets) — 24/7 recording, no gaps
- **NVR/VMS** correlates: Fixed cam records everything; PTZ bookmarks events with zoom

**Cost example (parking lot 200×300 ft):**
- 1× Dahua SD6AL230U-HNI (30×, AI tracking) ~$1,100

## PTZ Form Factors & Mounting

| Type | Pan / Tilt Range | Speed | Mount | Best For |
|------|------------------|-------|-------|----------|
| **Speed Dome** (hanging) | 360° endless / -15° to 90° | 240–400°/sec | Pendant (pole/ceiling) | Parking lots, intersections, stadiums |
| **Mini PTZ / Turret PTZ** | 355° / -5° to 90° | 60–100°/sec | Wall / ceiling / pole | Building corners, driveways, yards |
| **Positioning System** (heavy) | 360° endless / -90° to 90° | 100–200°/sec | Pedestal / tower | Perimeter, borders, critical infra |
| **PTZ Doorbell** | 350° / 90° | 30–60°/sec | Wall (doorbell wires) | Porch + driveway combo |

**Speed matters:** 400°/sec tracks a car at 30 mph at 150 ft. 60°/sec loses it. Check *preset tour speed* (not just max speed).

## Preset Tours & Patrols: "Virtual Guard" Mode

<Callout type="note">
**Preset:** Saved PTZ position (pan, tilt, zoom, focus). **Tour:** Sequence of presets with dwell time. **Pattern:** Recorded manual path (smooth motion).
</Callout>

**Typical tour for parking lot (8 presets, 15s dwell):**
1. Entrance gate (zoom 10×) — plate capture
2. Drive lane A (zoom 5×) — traffic flow
3. Handicap spots (zoom 8×) — compliance
4. Far corner (zoom 15×) — blind spot check
5. Exit gate (zoom 10×) — plate capture
6. Dumpster area (zoom 5×) — loitering
7. Building entrance (zoom 8×) — entry/exit
8. **Home** (wide, 1×) — overview

**Schedule tours by time:** Day = 5-min loops; Night = 2-min loops + IR on; Lunch rush = entrance-focused.

## Connectivity: PoE vs Wi-Fi vs 4G vs Fiber

| Power/Data | Max Distance | Reliability | Use Case |
|------------|--------------|-------------|----------|
| **PoE+ (802.3at, 30W)** | 328 ft (100 m) copper | ★★★★★ | Standard — use PoE injector/switch |
| **PoE++ (802.3bt, 60–90W)** | 328 ft | ★★★★★ | Heater/blower PTZ, IR illuminators |
| **Long-Range PoE (LRPoE)** | 1,500–3,000 ft | ★★★★☆ | Perimeter poles — needs LRPoE switch |
| **Fiber + Media Converter** | Miles | ★★★★★ | Campus, city, critical infra |
| **Wi-Fi 6 / 6E** | 150–300 ft LOS | ★★☆☆☆ | Temp installs, no cable path |
| **4G/5G LTE** | Anywhere with signal | ★★★☆☆ | Remote, construction, backup |

**Heater/Blower = PoE++ or 24V AC.** Most speed domes need 24V AC (separate transformer) or PoE++ for -40°F cold start. Check spec: `Heater: Yes` → budget 60W+.

## Storage & Bandwidth: PTZ Eats Both

| Stream | Resolution | Bitrate (H.265) | 24/7 Day (GB) | Notes |
|--------|------------|-----------------|---------------|-------|
| **Main (Recording)** | 4K / 8MP | 6–10 Mbps | 65–108 GB/day | Zoom doesn't change bitrate much |
| **Sub (Live/View)** | 1080p / 2MP | 1.5–3 Mbps | 16–32 GB/day | Used for auto-tracking detection |
| **Third (Mobile)** | 720p / D1 | 0.5–1 Mbps | 5–11 GB/day | Optional |

**PTZ-specific:** Preset tours = constant motion = **no P-frame savings**. Bitrate stays at I-frame peak. Budget 2× fixed-camera storage.

**NVR sizing:** 1× 4K PTZ + 3× 4K fixed = ~300 GB/day. 16 TB = 53 days. 32 TB = 106 days. Plan accordingly.

## Maintenance: The Hidden Cost

| Interval | Task | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| **Monthly** | Run full preset tour; verify focus at each zoom | Motors seize; focus drifts |
| **Quarterly** | Clean dome (microfiber + lens cleaner) | IR bounce, haze, spider webs |
| **Annually** | Check PTZ slip ring / cable harness | 360° endless = rotating connector wears |
| **2–3 Years** | Replace dome (polycarbonate yellows) | Light transmission loss = worse night |
| **5 Years** | Motor/gear rebuild or replace unit | Plastic gears strip; metal gears grind |

**Budget 10–15% of camera cost/year** for PTZ maintenance. Fixed cams: 1–2%.

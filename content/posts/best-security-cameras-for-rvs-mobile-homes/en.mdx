RV and mobile home security has unique constraints: 12V/24V DC power, constant vibration, -20°F to 140°F temps, limited roof space, and often no permanent internet. Standard home cameras fail fast. This guide covers cameras engineered for life on the move.

<Badge variant="outline">Key RV Difference</Badge> **No permanent wiring allowed** in most rentals/parks. Battery/solar + 12V DC hardwire are your only options. Wi-Fi is park Wi-Fi (unreliable) or cellular.

## RV-Specific Requirements Checklist

<Callout type="note">
**Must-haves for RV/mobile use:** 12V DC direct power (no AC adapter), vibration-rated mount, IP66+ waterproof, -4°F to 140°F operating range, cellular/LTE option or strong Wi-Fi antenna, microSD local recording (cloud fails off-grid).
</Callout>

| Feature | Why It Matters in an RV | Target Spec |
|---------|------------------------|-------------|
| Power Input | House batteries are 12V DC | 12V DC direct (no inverter loss) |
| Vibration Resistance | Highway shake kills standard mounts | MIL-STD-810G or equivalent |
| Temperature Range | Desert days, mountain nights | -20°F to 140°F (-29°C to 60°C) |
| Connectivity | Park Wi-Fi is congested/unsecured | 4G LTE / 5G + Wi-Fi dual mode |
| Storage | No internet = no cloud | microSD (128–512 GB) + loop recording |
| Mount | Can't drill roof on rental | Suction, magnetic, clamp, 3M VHB |

## Power Strategies for RVs

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="solar">
    <AccordionTrigger>Solar Panel Sizing & Placement</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**Rule of thumb:** Panel watts ≥ Camera draw (watts) × 3 (for winter/cloudy). Reolink Go PT Plus draws ~3W average → 10W panel minimum, 20W preferred.

**Mounting:**
- **Portable folding panel** (Renogy 20W, Jackery 20W): Set up at campsite, angle to sun, pack away. No roof holes.
- **Roof-mounted rigid** (Renogy 50W/100W): Permanent, charges house batteries → powers cameras via 12V bus. Needs charge controller.
- **Flexible semi-rigid** (SunPower 50W/100W): Curves to roof, lightweight, adhesives (3M VHB) — no screws.

**Pro tip:** Run panel → MPPT charge controller → house battery → 12V fuse block → camera 12V lines. Camera never sees panel directly.
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="12v">
    <AccordionTrigger>12V DC Hardwire (Cleanest for Permanent)</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
Tap RV house battery 12V system:
1. **Fuse block** (Blue Sea 5025/5026) near battery
2. **12V→5V buck converter** (for 5V cams) or **12V→48V PoE injector** (for PoE cams)
3. **Marine-grade wire** (18–16 AWG tinned copper) + **waterproof connectors** (Deutsch DT, SAE)
4. **Ignition-switched vs always-on:** Security cams = always-on (with low-voltage cutoff); Dash cams = ignition-switched

**Low-voltage cutoff:** Set at 11.8V (lead-acid) or 12.5V (LiFePO₄) to prevent killing house batteries.
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="battery">
    <AccordionTrigger>Battery-Only (No Solar, No Hardwire)</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
Use cameras with **large integrated batteries** + **low-power modes**:
- EufyCam 3: 13,000 mAh → ~6 months (motion only, no live view)
- Reolink Argus 4 Pro: 9,600 mAh → ~4 months
- Ring Stick Up Cam: 6,000 mAh → ~3 months

**Winter strategy:** Bring cams inside to charge monthly. Cold kills Li-ion capacity — a "6-month" cam lasts 6 weeks at 10°F.
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

<Callout type="warning">
**Never run AC inverter 24/7 for cameras.** 120V AC → 12V DC inverter draws 15–25W idle. That's 360–600 Wh/day — 30–50 Ah from your house bank. Use native 12V DC or 5V USB.
</Callout>

## Connectivity: Park Wi-Fi vs Cellular vs Starlink

| Scenario | Primary | Backup | Hardware |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|
| **Full-time, cellular coverage** | 4G/5G LTE camera (Reolink Go PT Plus) | Park Wi-Fi (travel router) | Camera + SIM ($10/mo) |
| **Weekend warrior, good parks** | Park Wi-Fi → Travel router (GL.iNet Beryl/Slate) | Phone hotspot | Router + cam Wi-Fi |
| **Remote/boondocking** | Starlink Roam / Mini | 4G camera | Starlink + 12V DC router |
| **Permanent park model** | Park Wi-Fi / Cable / Fiber | 4G failover | Ubiquiti Dream Router + LTE backup |

**Travel router hack:** GL.iNet GL-MT3000 (Beryl AX) or GL-SFT1200 (Opal) — USB powered, runs OpenWrt, connects to park Wi-Fi (captive portal via phone), creates private subnet for cameras, supports WireGuard/OpenVPN for remote NVR access.

## Mounting Without Drilling

| Mount Type | Best For | Hold Strength | Removal |
|------------|----------|---------------|---------|
| **3M VHB Tape** (5952, 4910) | Fiberglass/aluminum roof, smooth sides | 60+ lb/in² | Heat gun + floss — no residue |
| **Suction Cup** (SeaSucker, Ram Mount) | Glass, smooth gelcoat, temp mounts | 100+ lb | Release tab — instant |
| **Magnetic Base** (Strong ND magnets) | Steel roof/sides (Class A, some trailers) | 20–50 lb | Pull — instant |
| **Clamp / Rail Mount** | Ladder, awning arm, roof rack, window frame | 50+ lb | Unscrew — no marks |
| **Tripod / Pole** | Ground, picnic table, temporary | N/A | Pack up |

<Callout type="tip">
**Test before you trust.** Mount camera, drive 50 miles highway + 10 miles washboard. Check footage for vibration blur. Add rubber grommets / isolation washers if needed.
</Callout>

## Mobile Home / Park Model Specifics

If your home is stationary (skirted, utilities hooked up), you have more options:

- **PoE NVR kit** (Reolink RLK16-800D8, Amcrest NV4108E-HS) — run Cat6 in conduit under skirt
- **120V AC cameras** — standard outdoor cams work (Ring Floodlight, Google Nest Cam Outdoor, Arlo Pro 5S)
- **Trenching allowed** — bury direct-burial Cat6 or conduit to detached garage/shed
- **Park rules still apply** — submit ARA with "permanent residence" classification

Storage is where camera systems live or die. Lose footage = lose evidence. Cloud offers convenience but costs forever and risks privacy. Local (NVR, microSD, NAS) costs upfront but you own it. Hybrid gives both — if you configure it right. This guide breaks down every option with real 2026 pricing, bandwidth math, and decision frameworks.

<Badge variant="outline">Quick Answer</Badge> **Most homes:** PoE NVR (local 24/7) + cloud backup for alerts only. **Apartments/renters:** Camera with microSD + optional cloud sub. **Business:** NVR + NAS + offsite (Wasabi/Backblaze B2) + cloud alert clips.

## The Storage Landscape (2026)

| Category | Examples | Cost Model | Retention | Privacy | Internet Required |
|----------|----------|------------|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Cloud Subscription** | Ring Protect, Arlo Secure, Nest Aware, Eufy Cloud, Blink | $/cam/mo | 7–60 days | Vendor holds keys | Yes (24/7 upload) |

| **Cloud Object Storage** | Wasabi, Backblaze B2, AWS S3, Cloudflare R2 | $/TB/mo | Infinite | You encrypt | Yes (backup only) |
| **Hybrid** | UniFi Protect + Cloud Backup, Reolink NVR + FTP, Frigate + rclone | Mixed | Tiered | You control | Partial |

<Callout type="warning">
**Bandwidth Reality:** 8× 4K cameras @ 8 Mbps H.265 = 64 Mbps **continuous upload**. Most residential upload is 10–35 Mbps. **Full cloud 24/7 is impossible on typical internet.** Cloud = motion clips only, or sub-stream.
</Callout>

## Local Storage: NVR Deep Dive

### NVR vs DVR (2026: NVR Only)
- **DVR:** Analog coax (CVBS/AHD/TVI/CVI) → obsolete. Max 5MP. No PoE.
- **NVR:** IP cameras via Ethernet (PoE) → 4K/8K/12MP+, PoE, ONVIF, AI on NVR or camera.

### NVR Sizing Calculator

| Cameras | Resolution | Bitrate (H.265) | Daily GB | 14 Days | 30 Days | 90 Days | Recommended HDD |
|---------|------------|-----------------|----------|---------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| 4 | 4K (8MP) | 4 Mbps | 173 GB | 2.4 TB | 5.2 TB | 15.6 TB | 2× 8TB (RAID 1) |
| 8 | 4K | 4 Mbps | 346 GB | 4.8 TB | 10.4 TB | 31 TB | 4× 10TB (RAID 5/6) |
| 16 | 4K | 4 Mbps | 691 GB | 9.7 TB | 20.7 TB | 62 TB | 8× 10TB (RAID 6) |
| 8 | 5MP | 3 Mbps | 260 GB | 3.6 TB | 7.8 TB | 23 TB | 3× 8TB (RAID 5) |
| 16 | 2MP (1080p) | 1.5 Mbps | 130 GB | 1.8 TB | 3.9 TB | 11.7 TB | 2× 8TB (RAID 1) |

**Formula:** `GB/day = Cameras × Mbps × 86400 sec ÷ 8 ÷ 1024 ÷ 1024`

### RAID for NVR: What You Need

| RAID | Min Drives | Usable Capacity | Survives | Rebuild Risk | Best For |
|------|------------|-----------------|----------|--------------|----------|
| **RAID 1 (Mirror)** | 2 | 50% | 1 drive | Low | 2–4 bay, critical footage |
| **RAID 5** | 3 | (N-1)/N | 1 drive | Medium (URE) | 4–8 bay, balance |
| **RAID 6** | 4 | (N-2)/N | 2 drives | Low | 6+ bay, max protection |
| **RAID 10** | 4 (even) | 50% | 1–2 drives | Very Low | Performance + safety |
| **SHR (Synology)** | 2+ | Flexible | 1–2 | Low | Mixed drive sizes |

**Recommendation:** **RAID 6** for 6+ bays. **RAID 1** for 2-bay. Avoid RAID 5 on >8TB drives (URE risk during rebuild).

## microSD: Edge Storage Reality Check

| Card Spec | Sustained Write | 4K @ 15fps (4 Mbps) | 4K @ 30fps (8 Mbps) | 2K @ 15fps (3 Mbps) |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **UHS-I U3 / V30** (SanDisk Extreme, Samsung Pro) | 30 MB/s = 240 Mbps | ✅ 60× headroom | ✅ 30× headroom | ✅ 80× headroom |
| **UHS-I U1 / V10** (SanDisk Ultra, cheap) | 10 MB/s = 80 Mbps | ⚠️ 10× | ❌ 10× (drops frames) | ✅ 26× |
| **Application Class A1/A2** | Random IOPS optimized | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |

**Real-world limits:**
- **Heat:** 4K continuous write in sun-baked camera = card failure in 6–12 months
- **Endurance:** 128GB TLC = ~600 TBW. 4K @ 4 Mbps = 43 GB/day = **38 years theoretical** but heat cuts to 2–3 years
- **File system:** FAT32 (32GB limit) vs exFAT (no limit). Cameras format internally — trust their format.
- **Capacity sweet spot:** **128GB–256GB** (endurance + cost). 512GB+ = diminishing returns.

<Callout type="tip">
**microSD is NOT backup.** It's in the camera — theft/fire destroys both. Use for: buffer during internet outage, last 24–72h if NVR fails, rental/temp installs.
</Callout>

**Backup Strategy (rclone / duplicacy / restic):**
```
Local NVR (24/7) → Daily incremental to Wasabi (encrypted)
              → Weekly full to Backblaze B2 (geographic redundancy)
Retention: 90 days local, 1 year cloud, 7 years archive (Glacier IR)
```

<Callout type="note">
**Encrypt before upload.** Use `rclone crypt` or `restic` — your keys, your data. Provider never sees plaintext. Wasabi/B2/R2 all support SSE-C (customer-provided key) but client-side encryption is safer.
</Callout>

## Hybrid Architectures: Best of Both Worlds

### 1. UniFi Protect + Cloud Backup (Easiest Prosumer)
- **Local:** UNVR (7× HDD, RAID 6) → 24/7 4K recording
- **Cloud:** Protect Cloud Backup ($0.50/cam/mo) → event clips only
- **Alerts:** Push via UniFi cloud (no port forwarding)
- **Cost:** $1,000 hardware + $30/yr (5 cams) = **$1,150 Year 1, $30/yr after**

### 2. Reolink NVR + FTP/S3 Upload (Budget Hybrid)
- **Local:** RLN16-410 (16 ch, 2× SATA) → 24/7
- **Cloud:** NVR firmware → FTP/S3 (Wasabi/B2) on motion
- **Config:** Event-only upload, H.265, 7-day cloud retention
- **Cost:** $300 NVR + $84/yr Wasabi (1TB) = **$384 Year 1, $84/yr**

### 3. Frigate + rclone (DIY Max Control)
- **Local:** Frigate (Intel N100 + 4× 10TB RAID 5) → 24/7 + AI events
- **Cloud:** `rclone sync /media/frigate/clips wasabi:cctv-backup --encrypt` cron daily
- **Alerts:** Frigate MQTT → Home Assistant → Push/Telegram
- **Cost:** $600 hardware + $84/yr Wasabi = **$684 Year 1, $84/yr**

### 4. Synology Surveillance Station + C2 / Hyper Backup
- **Local:** DS923+ / DS1821+ + drives → 24/7 + AI
- **Cloud:** Hyper Backup → C2 Storage ($0.035/GB = $35/TB) or Wasabi/B2 via Cloud Sync
- **Cost:** $600 NAS + $100 drives + $35/yr = **$700 Year 1, $35/yr**

## Bandwidth Planning: The Hidden Killer

| Stream Type | Resolution | Codec | Bitrate | 8 Cams Upload | 16 Cams Upload |
|-------------|------------|-------|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Main (Recording)** | 4K | H.265 | 4–6 Mbps | 32–48 Mbps | 64–96 Mbps |
| **Sub (Live View)** | 1080p/D1 | H.265 | 0.5–1.5 Mbps | 4–12 Mbps | 8–24 Mbps |
| **Cloud Upload (Events)** | 1080p | H.265 | 1–2 Mbps | Burst 8–16 Mbps | Burst 16–32 Mbps |
| **Cloud Upload (24/7)** | 4K | H.265 | 4–6 Mbps | **32–48 Mbps constant** | **64–96 Mbps constant** |

**Residential Upload Reality:**
- Cable: 10–35 Mbps up
- Fiber (symmetrical): 300–1000 Mbps up
- DSL: 1–5 Mbps up
- Starlink: 10–25 Mbps up
- 5G Home: 20–100 Mbps up

**Rule:** If upload < 50 Mbps, **never** upload main stream 24/7. Use sub-stream for cloud backup, or event-only clips.

## Privacy & Legal: Who Holds the Keys?

| Storage | Encryption | Access by Vendor | Subpoena Risk | GDPR/CCPA |
|---------|------------|------------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Cloud (Ring/Arlo/Nest)** | At rest (vendor key) | Yes (decryptable) | High — vendor complies | Processor |
| **Cloud (Eufy)** | At rest (vendor key) | Optional (local-first) | Medium | Processor |
| **NVR Local** | None (or self-managed) | No | Low — you control | Controller |
| **NAS (Frigate/Scrypted)** | Self-managed (LUKS, VeraCrypt) | No | Low | Controller |
| **Object Storage (Wasabi/B2)** | SSE-S3 (vendor) / SSE-C (you) / Client-side (you) | Only if SSE-S3 | Medium (if vendor key) | Processor (if vendor key) |
| **Object Storage + Client Encrypt** | **You hold keys** | **Never** | **Minimal** | **Controller** |

**Best Practice:** Client-side encrypt (rclone crypt, restic, duplicacy) → upload to Wasabi/B2/R2. Vendor sees only encrypted blobs. You hold keys. Subpoena gets gibberish.

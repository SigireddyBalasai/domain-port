## Introducción: La Cámara Adecuada para Tus Necesidades

Comprar una cámara de seguridad puede ser abrumador. Docenas de marcas, innumerables especificaciones, términos técnicos confusos – y la elección incorrecta significa dinero desperdiciado y protección inadecuada.

Esta guía te lleva paso a paso a través de las decisiones más importantes: ¿Dónde irá la cámara? ¿Qué debe monitorear? ¿Cuál es tu presupuesto? Al final, sabrás exactamente qué cámara es la adecuada para ti.

## Step 1: Determine the Location

The most important question first: Where will the camera be installed?

**Indoor:**

- Small cameras, USB power, freestanding or adhesive mount
- No weather resistance needed (IP20/IP30 is sufficient)
- Privacy is important — physical privacy switch recommended
- Examples: Living room, hallway, kids' room, office

**Outdoor (sheltered):**

- Under eaves, in entryways, covered patios
- IP65 is sufficient (protection against splashing water)
- WiFi or PoE possible

**Outdoor (fully exposed):**

- Garden, driveway, property boundary
- IP66 or IP67 required
- PoE recommended (more reliable than WiFi)
- Solar or battery for wireless options

<Callout type="info">

**Rule of thumb:** Sheltered = IP65, Fully exposed = IP66+, Indoor = IP20 is sufficient.

</Callout>

## Step 2: Define the Purpose

What should the camera capture?

**General surveillance:**

- Who comes and goes
- Rough overview of the area
- 1080p sufficient

**Identification (faces/license plates):**

- Capture details at 5-10 meters
- 2K recommended, 4K for longer distances
- Good lighting or good night vision required

**Deterrence:**

- Visible camera
- Spotlight or siren integrated
- Motion-activated lighting

**Special applications:**

- Pet monitoring: Two-way audio, pan/tilt function
- Baby monitor: Low latency, night vision, temperature sensor
- Package monitoring: Motion detection + privacy zones

## Step 4: Choose Resolution

| Resolution      | Pixels    | Megapixels | Recommended Use                  |
| --------------- | --------- | ---------- | -------------------------------- |
| 1080p (Full HD) | 1920x1080 | 2 MP       | Indoor, small outdoor areas      |
| 2K (QHD)        | 2560x1440 | 4 MP       | Outdoor, face detection          |
| 4K (Ultra HD)   | 3840x2160 | 8 MP       | Large properties, license plates |

## Step 5: Decide on Storage

**Local Storage (microSD/NVR):**

- No monthly costs
- Data in your hands
- Check microSD capacity (128-512 GB)
- NVR for multi-camera systems

**Cloud Storage:**

- Automatic backups
- Access from anywhere
- Monthly fees ($3-15/month)
- Often combined with AI features

**Recommendation:** Prefer local storage, use cloud as optional backup.

## Step 6: Determine Power Supply

| Power Type | Advantages                 | Disadvantages          | Ideal For              |
| ---------- | -------------------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------- |
| USB        | Simple, affordable         | Visible cable          | Indoor                 |
| Battery    | Wireless, flexible         | Needs charging         | Rental apartments      |
| Solar      | Unlimited, self-sufficient | Sun required           | Outdoor, garden        |
| PoE        | Very reliable              | Cable routing required | Permanent installation |

## Step 7: Smart Features

**Motion Detection:**

- Basic: Any motion (many false alarms)
- PIR: Heat-based (fewer false alarms)
- AI: People/vehicles/animals (best detection)

**Night Vision:**

- IR: Standard, black & white, up to 15 meters
- Color Night Vision: With spotlight, color, shorter range
- Starlight: Sensitive sensor, color with low ambient light

**Audio:**

- Two-Way Audio: Speak and listen
- Siren: Loud alarm for deterrence
- Sound Detection: Reacts to glass break, smoke alarm

## Step 8: App and Ecosystem

The app is your daily interface to the camera. Look for:

- User-friendliness
- Reliable push notifications
- Privacy settings
- Integration with Alexa/Google Home/HomeKit
- Firmware updates

## Step 9: Privacy and Security

<Callout type="warning">

**Security Checklist:** - Change default password immediately - Enable two-factor authentication - Update firmware regularly - Place cameras on a separate WiFi network - Configure privacy zones - Use physical privacy switch (if available)

</Callout>

## Step 10: Test and Adjust

After purchase:

1. Test camera indoors first
2. Check WiFi reception at the installation location
3. Adjust camera angle
4. Configure motion zones
5. Optimize notification settings
6. Readjust after a week

## Avoid Common Mistakes

**Too wide angle:** Ultra-wide cameras (180°) distort details at the edges. A 100-130° camera is ideal for most applications.

**Wrong height:** Cameras mounted too high won't detect faces. 2.5-3 meters is ideal.

**Backlight:** Don't aim the camera toward the sun or bright windows.

**WiFi range:** Check WiFi reception at the installation location before mounting.

**No motion zones:** Without configured zones, you'll get hundreds of false alarms per day.

Start small. One camera, tested indoors, then installed at the final location. After a week you'll know if you need more. The **Wyze Cam v4** (indoor) and **Reolink Argus 3 Pro** (outdoor) are ideal starter cameras.

Homeowners Associations control what you can mount, where you can point it, and even what color it can be. Violations mean fines, forced removal, and neighborhood friction. This guide covers cameras that fly under the radar—literally and figuratively—while keeping your property secure.

<Badge variant="outline">Critical First Step</Badge> **Read your CC&Rs and
Architectural Guidelines before buying.** Every HOA differs. Some ban all
exterior cameras. Others allow only specific models, colors, or mounting
locations. Submit an Architectural Review Application (ARA) with photos and
specs *before* installation.

## Common HOA Restrictions & How to Meet Them

<Callout type="note">

**Typical HOA rules you'll encounter:** Camera must be "architecturally
compatible" (match trim/soffit color), dome form factor only, no view into
neighbor's yard/windows, wireless preferred (no visible conduit), max height
limits, approved vendor lists.

</Callout>

| Restriction                   | Compliant Solution                            | Cameras That Fit                                                               |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Color match required**      | Paintable housings, white/beige/black options | Reolink E1 Outdoor (white), EufyCam 3 (white), Ring Stick Up Cam (white/black) |
| **Dome/turret only**          | No bullet "gun" profiles                      | Reolink RLC-511WA, Amcrest AD410, Ubiquiti G4 Dome                             |
| **No neighbor view**          | Privacy masks + narrow FOV + strategic aim    | Any camera with privacy zones (Reolink, Amcrest, Ring, Eufy)                   |
| **Wireless only**             | Battery/solar, no exterior conduit            | EufyCam 3, Reolink Argus 4 Pro, Ring Stick Up Cam Battery                      |
| **Height limit (e.g., 8 ft)** | Eave/soffit mount, low-profile dome           | Reolink E1 Outdoor, Ubiquiti G4 Doorbell, Ring Floodlight Cam Wired Pro        |
| **Approved vendor list**      | Check list; request exception with specs      | Document camera specs, safety ratings, color samples for appeal                |

## Architectural Review Package Checklist

Submit this with your ARA for fastest approval:

- [ ] **Camera spec sheet** (1 page: model, dimensions, color, weight, power, wireless/PoE)
- [ ] **Mounting photos** — mock up with painter's tape or print-to-scale paper cutout on house
- [ ] **Field of view diagram** — sketch property, show camera cones, mark privacy masks over neighbors
- [ ] **Color samples** — photo of camera next to your trim/soffit/gutter
- [ ] **Wiring plan** — "Single Cat6 to attic" or "Battery/solar, no exterior wiring"
- [ ] **Recording policy** — "Local storage only, 14-day retention, no cloud sharing"
- [ ] **Neighbor sign-off** (if required) — adjacent owners acknowledge no view into their property

<Callout type="warning">

**Don't install first, ask forgiveness.** HOAs can fine daily ($50–500/day),
force removal at your cost, and lien the property. The ARA process takes 2–4
weeks. Plan accordingly.

</Callout>

## If Your Request Is Denied: Appeal Strategy

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="appeal-1">
    <AccordionTrigger>
      Request written denial with specific guideline citations
    </AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>

      Boards must cite the exact CC&R section or Architectural Guideline. Vague
      "aesthetic concerns" are challengeable. Get it in writing.

    </AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="appeal-2">
    <AccordionTrigger>Propose compromises in writing</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>

      Offer: paint housing to match, add privacy masks, reduce camera count, use
      specific model board pre-approved elsewhere in community, relocate to less
      visible spot.

    </AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="appeal-3">
    <AccordionTrigger>Cite state law protections</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>

      Many states (CA, FL, TX, AZ, CO, others) limit HOA ability to ban security
      cameras. California Civil Code §4740: "Governing documents cannot prohibit
      security cameras." Check your state.

    </AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="appeal-4">
    <AccordionTrigger>Request variance / exception hearing</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>

      Present at board meeting with photos, neighbor support letters, police
      report (if crime occurred), insurance discount proof. Frame as safety, not
      surveillance.

    </AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

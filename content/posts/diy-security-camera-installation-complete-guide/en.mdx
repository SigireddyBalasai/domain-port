Professional security camera installation costs **$150–$400 per camera** (labor + materials). A typical 8-camera PoE system: **$1,200–$3,200 labor**. DIY with this guide: **$0 labor + $100–$300 tools**. This guide covers every step from planning to first recording.

## Phase 1: Plan & Design (Do This First)

### 1. Camera Placement Map

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="placement">
    <AccordionTrigger>Where to Place Cameras (Priority Order)</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**Tier 1 — Must Cover (Entry/Exit):**
- Front door (doorbell or overhead)
- Back/side doors
- Garage entry (pedestrian + vehicle)
- Driveway approach (license plates)

**Tier 2 — High Value (Coverage):**
- First-floor windows (ground access)
- Backyard / patio (theft, vandalism)
- Side gates / fence gaps
- Package drop zone

**Tier 3 — Situational:**
- Pool / hot tub (safety + liability)
- Shed / detached garage
- Mailbox (theft)
- Street view (neighborhood context)

**Placement Rules:**
- **Height:** 8–10 ft (out of reach, good angle)
- **Angle:** 15–30° down from horizontal
- **FOV Overlap:** 20–30% between adjacent cameras
- **No Backlight:** Avoid pointing at rising/setting sun, bright lights
- **Weather:** Under eave/soffit when possible; IP66+ if exposed
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

### 2. Choose Your Architecture

| Architecture | Best For | Cable Runs | Power | Difficulty |
|--------------|----------|------------|-------|------------|
| **PoE NVR Kit** | 4–16 cams, permanent, 24/7 | Cat5e/6 to each cam | PoE (switch/NVR) | Medium |
| **Wi-Fi + NVR** | 2–8 cams, hard-to-wire spots | Power only (outlet/solar) | 120V / Battery / Solar | Low–Medium |
| **Battery/Solar Only** | 1–4 cams, no wires, rentals | None | Battery + Solar panel | Low |
| **Hybrid** | Most homes | Mix | Mix | Medium |

<Callout type="tip">
**Start with PoE where you can.** It's the most reliable, highest quality, and lowest long-term cost. Use Wi-Fi/battery only where cable is truly impractical.
</Callout>

### 3. Cable Route Planning (PoE)

- **Interior walls:** Drill top plate, fish down to basement/crawl, up to attic
- **Exterior walls:** Drill through rim joist or sill plate, seal with silicone
- **Attic routes:** Run along rafters, staple every 4 ft, avoid insulation contact
- **Conduit:** ¾" EMT or PVC for exposed runs (code in many areas)
- **Max length:** 328 ft (100 m) per Cat5e/6 run. Use PoE extender or fiber beyond.

### 4. Tool & Material Checklist

**Essential Tools:**
- [ ] Cordless drill/driver (18V+)
- [ ] ½" spade bit / ⅞" auger bit (cable holes)
- [ ] Fish tape / glow rods (25–50 ft)
- [ ] Stud finder (AC sensing)
- [ ] Level (torpedo or laser)
- [ ] Ladder (extension for 2-story)
- [ ] Crimp tool (RJ45 pass-through) + cable tester
- [ ] Cable stripper (Cat5e/6)
- [ ] Silicone sealant (exterior grade)
- [ ] Cable staples / J-hooks / zip ties

**Materials (PoE):**
- [ ] Cat6 solid copper (CMR riser for in-wall, CMX outdoor for exterior)
- [ ] RJ45 pass-through connectors (50-pack)
- [ ] Keystone jacks + wall plates (optional, for clean terminations)
- [ ] PoE switch (8/16/24 port, 802.3at/bt) or NVR with PoE ports
- [ ] UPS for NVR + switch (1500VA min)

**Materials (Wireless/Battery):**
- [ ] Mounting brackets (included or universal)
- [ ] Solar panels + mounting arms
- [ ] 120V outdoor outlets / weatherproof boxes (if plug-in)

## Phase 2: Mounting Hardware & Techniques

### Wall Mount (Stucco, Brick, Siding, Vinyl)

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="wall-mount">
    <AccordionTrigger>Surface-Specific Mounting</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**Vinyl / Aluminum Siding:**
- Use **vinyl siding mounting block** (J-channel compatible) — no drilling into siding
- Or: 3M VHB tape + screw into sheathing behind (locate stud)

**Stucco / Brick / Concrete:**
- **Hammer drill + ¼" masonry bit** → Plastic anchors (red/blue) + stainless screws
- **Tapcon screws** (blue) directly into masonry — no anchor needed
- **Adhesive option:** 3M VHB 5952 + mechanical screw (redundancy)

**Wood / Hardie / LP SmartSide:**
- Pilot hole → **#8–#10 stainless/coated deck screw** 2–2.5" into stud
- Use **mounting block** (1×4 or PVC) for flat surface on lap siding

**Eave / Soffit (Best Location):**
- Screw into **rafter tail** or **soffit vent framing** — not just thin soffit panel
- Use **1.5–2" screw** into solid wood
- Angle bracket if soffit slopes

**Universal Pro Tip:** **Always hit structure.** Camera + wind = lever. 4 screws into stud/rafter > 8 screws into sheathing only.
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

### Ceiling / Soffit / Eave Mount

- **Junction box** (weatherproof, 4" round) → camera bracket screws to box
- **Conduit** (½" EMT or LFMC) from box to camera if cable exposed
- **Seal:** Silicone around box penetration + cable entry

### Pole Mount (Driveway, Gate, Yard)

- **Schedule 40 steel pipe** (1.5" or 2" ID) → set in concrete (18" deep × 8" dia)
- **U-bolt bracket** for camera
- **Conduit** up pole interior or surface-mounted LFMC
- **Grounding:** #6 CU to ground rod at pole base (NEC 250.52)

## Phase 3: Cable Running (PoE Systems)

### Interior Fish (Attic → Wall → Camera)

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="cable-run">
    <AccordionTrigger>Step-by-Step: Attic to Exterior Camera</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
1. **Locate camera position** from inside (attic) — measure from known landmark (vent, chimney)
2. **Drill ⅞" hole** through top plate (interior wall) or soffit (eave mount)
3. **Push fish tape** from attic down wall cavity to camera hole
4. **Attach Cat6** to fish tape: fold 6" back, tape smooth, pull **gently**
5. **Leave 3–4 ft slack** at camera end (service loop)
6. **Seal exterior hole:** Silicone around cable + grommet
7. **Label both ends:** "CAM-01 Front Door" (Sharpie + cable label)
8. **Test continuity** with cable tester before terminating
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

### Termination: RJ45 Pass-Through (Fastest, Most Reliable)

<Callout type="note">
**Use pass-through connectors.** Standard RJ45 requires precise wire trim. Pass-through lets you verify color order *before* crimping. **T568B standard** (most common):
</Callout>

| Pin | Color | Pin | Color |
|-----|-------|-----|-------|
| 1 | Orange/White | 5 | Blue/White |
| 2 | Orange | 6 | Green |
| 3 | Green/White | 7 | Brown/White |
| 4 | Blue | 8 | Brown |

**Crimp Process:**
1. Strip 1.5" jacket → untwist pairs → arrange T568B
2. Insert into pass-through → verify colors visible at front
3. Trim flush with connector face
4. Crimp **firmly** (ratchet tool clicks once)
5. **Test every cable** — all 8 LEDs green = good

### PoE Switch / NVR Connection

- **Home run** each camera to switch/NVR (not daisy-chain)
- **Patch panel** (optional but pro): 12/24-port keystone → short patch cables to switch
- **UPS:** NVR + PoE switch on battery backup. Cameras stay powered during outage.

## Phase 4: Wireless / Battery Camera Setup

### Wi-Fi Optimization

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="wifi-opt">
    <AccordionTrigger>Get Reliable Wi-Fi to Cameras</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**Placement:**
- Camera within **50 ft** of router/AP (through 2 walls max)
- Avoid metal siding, chimney, HVAC duct between cam and AP

**Hardware Upgrades:**
- **Mesh system** (Eero Pro 6E, UniFi Dream Router, Asus ZenWiFi) — wired backhaul preferred
- **Outdoor AP** (UniFi U6 Mesh, Ubiquiti UAP-AC-M) — PoE powered, mounted under eave
- **Directional antenna** (2.4 GHz Yagi) for long-range single camera

**Channel Planning:**
- 2.4 GHz: Channels 1, 6, 11 only (non-overlapping)
- Force camera to 2.4 GHz (better penetration) — disable 5 GHz in camera app
- Set router 2.4 GHz to **20 MHz width** (not 40) for stability

**Test:** Phone at camera location → Speedtest → Need **>5 Mbps upload** per camera (1080p) / **>15 Mbps** (4K)
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

### Battery / Solar Commissioning

1. **Charge fully** before mount (12–24 hrs on USB-C)
2. **Pair to app** at install height (test Wi-Fi signal)
3. **Mount bracket** → attach camera → adjust angle
4. **Solar panel:** Face **true south** (NH), tilt = latitude ±15° (winter +15°, summer -15°)
5. **Verify charging:** App shows ☀️ / ⚡ icon within 1 hr of sun
6. **Set schedules:** Motion-only at night; disable live view auto-start

## Phase 5: NVR / VMS Configuration

### Initial Setup (Reolink / Amcrest / Dahua / Hikvision / UniFi)

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="nvr-setup">
    <AccordionTrigger>NVR First Boot Checklist</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
1. **Install HDDs** before power (SATA, WD Purple / Seagate SkyHawk / Toshiba S300)
2. **Power on** → Wizard: Date/Time (NTP), Language, Password (strong!)
3. **Network:** Static IP for NVR (e.g., 192.168.1.50), Gateway, DNS (1.1.1.1)
4. **PoE Ports:** Enable all → "Auto Add" cameras (plug cameras in one by one)
5. **Encode Settings (per camera):**
   - **Main Stream:** 4K/8MP @ 15–20 fps, H.265, VBR, 4–6 Mbps
   - **Sub Stream:** 720p/D1 @ 15 fps, H.265, 0.5–1 Mbps (mobile live view)
6. **Record Schedule:** Continuous 24/7 on Main; Motion on Sub (saves space)
7. **Motion Zones:** Draw only relevant areas (exclude trees, street, clouds)
8. **Smart Events:** Enable Person/Vehicle detection (reduces false alerts 90%)
9. **Storage:** Format HDD → Set overwrite (oldest first) → Enable S.M.A.R.T. alerts
10. **Remote Access:** **DO NOT PORT FORWARD.** Use:
    - **Vendor Cloud** (Reolink, Dahua, Hikvision, UniFi) — easiest
    - **VPN** (WireGuard on router, Tailscale, ZeroTier) — most secure
    - **Tailscale Funnel / Cloudflare Tunnel** — advanced, no VPN client needed
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

## Phase 6: Network Security (Critical)

<Callout type="danger">
**Never port-forward camera/NVR ports (80, 443, 8000, 554, 8080).** Shodan/Censys scan 24/7. Default creds = botnet in minutes.
</Callout>

**Secure Remote Access Options (Best → Good):**

| Method | Security | Ease | Cost | Platform |
|--------|----------|------|------|----------|
| **Vendor Cloud (UniFi, Reolink, Eufy, Ring)** | Good (TLS, auth) | Easiest | Free | All |
| **Tailscale / ZeroTier** (VPN mesh) | Excellent (WireGuard) | Medium | Free (personal) | All |
| **WireGuard on Router** (OpenWrt, OPNsense, pfSense) | Excellent | Harder | Free | All |
| **Cloudflare Tunnel** (Zero Trust) | Excellent | Medium | Free | Web UI only |
| **OpenVPN Server on Router** | Good | Medium | Free | All |

**Camera-Level Hardening:**
- Change **admin password** (16+ chars, password manager)
- Disable **ONVIF** if not used
- Disable **UPnP** on router
- Enable **HTTPS only** (self-signed cert OK for local)
- **Firmware update** quarterly (vendor site, not auto)

## Phase 7: Testing & Commissioning

### Camera-by-Camera Verification

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="testing">
    <AccordionTrigger>Complete Test Checklist</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**Daytime:**
- [ ] Live view: Clear, no artifacts, correct orientation
- [ ] FOV: Covers intended zone, no blind spots
- [ ] Focus: Sharp at target distance (license plate at 30 ft)
- [ ] Motion detection: Walk test → alert <5 sec
- [ ] Smart events: Person/Vehicle/Package trigger correctly
- [ ] Recording: Continuous + motion both writing to HDD

**Nighttime (Critical):**
- [ ] IR illuminators on (red glow visible)
- [ ] No IR reflection (glass, white siding, overhang)
- [ ] No "whiteout" from nearby lights
- [ ] Plate readable at target distance
- [ ] Color night vision (if supported) — needs ambient light

**Network:**
- [ ] Camera IP reachable from PC (ping)
- [ ] RTSP stream playable in VLC (`rtsp://user:pass@ip:554/stream1`)
- [ ] NVR recording 24/7 no gaps (check timeline)
- [ ] Remote access works (phone on LTE, not Wi-Fi)

**Physical:**
- [ ] All screws tight, camera doesn't wobble
- [ ] Cable loops secured (no wind whip)
- [ ] Silicone seals intact (no water ingress)
- [ ] Conduit fittings tight
- [ ] Ground wire connected (pole mounts)

## Troubleshooting Quick Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| **No image / "No Signal"** | PoE power budget exceeded | Check switch PoE budget; move to injector |
| **Image flicker / rolling lines** | Ground loop / bad ground | Common ground for all equipment; isolator |
| **Night vision whiteout** | IR reflection | Angle camera down; move away from wall/eave; disable IR + external illuminator |
| **Motion alerts constant** | Trees, shadows, rain, bugs | Narrow zones; enable Person/Vehicle only; reduce sensitivity |
| **Wi-Fi drops** | Channel congestion / distance | 2.4 GHz only; 20 MHz; mesh node near camera; directional antenna |
| **NVR beeping** | HDD failure / full / fan | Check S.M.A.R.T.; replace drive; clean fan |
| **Remote access fails** | ISP CGNAT / double NAT | Use Tailscale / Cloudflare Tunnel / Vendor Cloud |
| **Plate unreadable at night** | Shutter too slow / IR weak | Increase shutter (1/100+); add external IR illuminator; 4K + optical zoom |

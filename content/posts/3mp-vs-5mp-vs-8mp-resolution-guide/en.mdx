Choosing the right camera resolution is one of the most important decisions when planning a surveillance system. 3MP, 5MP and 8MP (4K) are the most common options in 2026, but more megapixels do not automatically mean better security footage.

This guide compares the three resolution tiers across every relevant category: pixel count, identification distance, storage demand, night-vision suitability, and cost per camera.

<Callout type="info" title="The key point up front">

**There is no universally best resolution.** 8MP delivers the most detail but
needs more storage and light. 5MP offers the best compromise between detail
and storage use. 3MP is perfectly adequate for most indoor spaces and is the
most cost-effective.

</Callout>

## Resolutions compared

| Specification            | 3MP         | 5MP         | 8MP (4K)     |
| ------------------------ | ----------- | ----------- | ------------ |
| Resolution               | 2304 × 1296 | 2560 × 1920 | 3840 × 2160  |
| Pixels (total)           | ~3 million  | ~5 million  | ~8.3 million |
| Aspect ratio             | 16:9        | 4:3         | 16:9         |
| Pixels per foot (at 12m) | 48          | 64          | 96           |
| ID distance (DORI)       | 6–9 m       | 9–15 m      | 15–25 m      |
| Storage/day (H.265)      | 8–12 GB     | 25–35 GB    | 30–50 GB     |
| Relative cost            | 1×          | 1.3–1.5×    | 1.5–2.5×     |

### 3MP (2304 × 1296) — the solid baseline

3MP is the successor to 1080p (2MP) and offers around 50% more pixels. This resolution is ideal for:

- **Indoor spaces** — offices, corridors, warehouses
- **Close-range surveillance** up to 6–9 meters
- **Budget-conscious installs** with many cameras
- **Wi-Fi cameras** with limited bandwidth

Advantages: low storage use, good night vision thanks to larger pixels, low cost.
Disadvantages: limited zoom capability, shorter identification distance.

### 5MP (2560 × 1920) — the all-rounder

5MP uses a 4:3 aspect ratio that provides more vertical coverage than 16:9. This makes 5MP especially suitable for:

- **Outdoor areas** — entrances, parking lots, driveways
- **Hallways and corridors** — where vertical coverage matters
- **Areas with medium identification distance** (9–15 meters)
- **Well-lit scenes**

Advantages: excellent 4:3 format for vertical scenes, good detail, fair storage use.
Disadvantages: weaker night vision than 3MP at the same sensor size; no 16:9 for widescreen monitors.

### 8MP (3840 × 2160 / 4K) — the flagship

4K (8MP) offers the highest detail density and enables digital zooms that would be blurry at lower resolutions. Ideal for:

- **Wide outdoor areas** — large parking lots, storage yards, entrance zones
- **License plate and face recognition** at distance (15–25 meters)
- **Forensic detail captures** — zooming into the image after the fact
- **Areas that must be covered without gaps**

Advantages: maximum detail, best digital zoom, future-proof.
Disadvantages: highest storage use, weaker night vision on small sensors, higher cost.

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch out for 4K night vision">

Many cheap 4K cameras use small sensors (1/3" or 1/2.7"), which causes heavy
image noise at dusk. A 5MP camera with a 1/1.8" sensor often delivers the
clearer image in poor light. Always check the sensor size, not just the
megapixel count!

</Callout>

## Storage consumption in detail

Storage use is often the deciding factor when choosing a resolution:

| Scenario                  | 3MP         | 5MP          | 8MP          |
| ------------------------- | ----------- | ------------ | ------------ |
| Continuous H.265          | 8–12 GB/day | 25–35 GB/day | 30–50 GB/day |
| Continuous H.265+         | 5–8 GB/day  | 15–22 GB/day | 20–35 GB/day |
| Motion H.265              | 2–4 GB/day  | 7–12 GB/day  | 10–18 GB/day |
| 8-camera system (30 days) | 0.7–1.1 TB  | 2.1–3.0 TB   | 2.5–4.2 TB   |

<Callout type="tip" title="Save storage with H.265+">

H.265+ (also called Smart H.265+) analyzes the scene and encodes only moving
areas at full resolution. Static backgrounds are stored at a reduced bitrate.
This saves 30–50% of storage space with no visible loss of quality. Enable
H.265+ on your NVR or recorder — the feature is available by default from most
manufacturers.

</Callout>

## Night vision and light sensitivity

Sensor size is often more important for night-vision performance than megapixel count:

| Sensor             | 3MP       | 5MP       | 8MP      |
| ------------------ | --------- | --------- | -------- |
| 1/3" (standard)    | Good      | Adequate  | Weak     |
| 1/2.8"             | Very good | Good      | Adequate |
| 1/1.8" (Starlight) | Excellent | Very good | Good     |

Rule of thumb: **for each resolution step up, you need roughly one sensor-size step up** to achieve the same night-vision performance. A 3MP camera with a 1/3" sensor sees about as well at night as a 5MP camera with a 1/2.8" sensor.

## Recommendations by location

| Location                 | Recommended resolution | Reason                                         |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| House entrance / door    | 5MP or 8MP             | Face recognition at short distance             |
| Driveway / parking lot   | 5MP or 8MP             | Plates at 10–20 m                              |
| Living room / office     | 3MP                    | Perfectly adequate, low storage                |
| Hallway / corridor       | 5MP (4:3)              | Optimal vertical coverage                      |
| Warehouse / workshop     | 3MP or 5MP             | Overview, large pixels for better night vision |
| Large parking lot / yard | 8MP (4K)               | Maximum detail coverage                        |

<Callout type="note" title="Practical recommendation">

For most homes, a mix of 3MP indoor cameras and 5MP outdoor cameras is
perfectly sufficient. 8MP (4K) makes sense when you must identify faces or
plates beyond 15 meters — or when storage budget is no concern.

</Callout>

## Conclusion

3MP, 5MP and 8MP each have their place. Plan your surveillance system on the DORI principle: **choose the resolution based on the identification distance you need**, not the highest available number.

- **3MP** — indoor spaces, close range, budget-friendly
- **5MP** — outdoor areas, all-rounder, vertical coverage
- **8MP (4K)** — wide areas, maximum detail, high storage demand

The best surveillance system often uses all three resolutions where each delivers the most benefit.

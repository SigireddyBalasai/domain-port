Two-way audio turns a camera into an intercom: talk to delivery drivers, deter intruders, check on kids/pets, or communicate with employees. But not all "two-way audio" is equal — half-duplex (walkie-talkie) vs full-duplex (phone call), noise cancellation quality, and legal consent requirements vary wildly.

<Badge variant="outline">Key Distinction</Badge> **Half-duplex** = push-to-talk,
one direction at a time (most budget cams). **Full-duplex** = simultaneous
talk/listen like a phone (premium cams, dedicated intercoms).

**MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems)** — Standard in modern cams. Tiny, durable, flat frequency response.

<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>Omnidirectional</strong> — Picks up 360°; good for room coverage,
    bad for focused pickup{" "}
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Directional (beamforming array)</strong> — 2–4 MEMS + DSP steering;
    focuses on voice, rejects background
  </li>
</ul>

<p>
  <strong>Placement matters:</strong>
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Doorbell: Bottom face, angled down → captures visitor at 3–5 ft</li>
  <li>Turret/Dome: Under lens or side → captures 10–20 ft radius</li>
  <li>Bullet: Rear/side vent → often muffled by housing</li>
  <li>Indoor: Top front → best for room conversation</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Wind/weather:</strong> Look for <strong>acoustic mesh</strong> (Gore-Tex / ePTFE) over mic port — blocks water, passes sound. Cheap cams skip this → rain = static.</p>
</AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>

<p>
  <strong>Typical specs:</strong> 0.5–2W Class D amp + 28–40mm driver → 80–100
  dB SPL @ 1 ft
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Real-world:</strong> 85 dB = comfortable conversation at 6 ft; 95 dB =
  audible across 30 ft yard
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Power supply matters:</strong>
</p>

<ul>
  <li>PoE (802.3af 15.4W) → plenty for 2W audio + camera</li>
  <li>12V DC → fine</li>
  <li>
    Battery cams → <strong>audio kills battery</strong> (amp draws 200–500 mA).
    Ring/Eufy limit talk time to 30–60s on battery.
  </li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Distortion:</strong> Cheap Class D amps clip at max volume. Look for <strong>THD &lt;1% @ 1W</strong> or "HD Audio" marketing (usually means better amp + DSP).</p>
</AccordionContent>

<p>
  <strong>Echo Cancellation (AEC) is the differentiator:</strong>
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Half-duplex doesn't need AEC (mic muted while speaker plays)</li>
  <li>
    Full-duplex <strong>requires</strong> adaptive AEC (NLMS/RLS/Kalman) —
    without it, you hear your own voice delayed = unusable
  </li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Test before buying:</strong> Call the camera from app, speak while it's playing. If you hear echo/feedback → AEC fails.</p>

## Noise Cancellation: What Actually Works

<Callout type="note">

**DSP Chain in Good Cameras:** Beamforming → Stationary Noise Suppression
(Wiener/Kalman) → Non-Stationary Suppression (DNN) → AEC (full-duplex) → AGC
(auto gain) → Limiter

</Callout>

| Noise Type              | Suppression Method                         | Typical Reduction |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ----------------- |
| Wind                    | Physical mesh + spectral gating            | 15–25 dB          |
| HVAC / Fan / Traffic    | Spectral subtraction / Wiener              | 10–20 dB          |
| Dog bark / Kid scream   | DNN-based non-stationary (RNNoise, NSNet2) | 15–30 dB          |
| Your own voice (echo)   | Adaptive AEC (NLMS/Kalman)                 | 25–40 dB          |
| Speaker feedback (howl) | Notch filtering + gain margin              | Prevention        |

**Marketing vs Reality:** "AI Noise Cancellation" = usually just spectral gate. **Real DNN noise suppression** needs NPU (Ambarella CV25, Rockchip RV1126, Google TPU, Apple Neural Engine). Check specs: "RNNoise", "NSNet2", "DNN-based", "Deep learning noise reduction."

## Legal: One-Party vs Two-Party Consent

<Callout type="warning">

**Audio recording laws are stricter than video.** Video = reasonable
expectation of privacy (varies). Audio = wiretap statutes in many states.
**Consent requirements apply to _recording_, not live listen.**

</Callout>

### US State Consent Laws (2026)

| Consent Type                          | States                                                                                                                                                     | What It Means                                                          |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **One-Party** (38 states + DC)        | AL, AK, AZ, AR, CO, DE, DC, GA, HI, ID, IN, IA, KS, KY, LA, ME, MN, MS, MO, NE, NV, NJ, NM, NY, NC, ND, OH, OK, RI, SC, SD, TN, TX, UT, VT, VA, WV, WI, WY | You can record if _you_ are a party to conversation. No notice needed. |
| **Two-Party / All-Party** (12 states) | CA, CT, FL, IL, MD, MA, MI, MT, NH, OR, PA, WA                                                                                                             | **All parties must consent.** Signage + verbal notice required.        |
| **Hybrid / Complex**                  | VT (one-party but strict), OR (all-party for in-person)                                                                                                    | Check local counsel                                                    |

**Federal (Wiretap Act):** One-party consent baseline. State law can be stricter.

## Top Cameras by Use Case (2026)

### 1. Best Full-Duplex Doorbell: Google Nest Doorbell (Wired, 2nd Gen)

**Audio:** Full-duplex, excellent AEC, 2W speaker, 3-mic array with beamforming  
**Noise Cancel:** TPU-accelerated DNN (Google silicon) — best-in-class wind/traffic rejection  
**Consent Features:** "Visitor Announcement" (chime + "Someone's at the door" before recording); Audio recording toggle in Home app  
**Integration:** Google Home, Assistant, Nest Aware ($8/mo for 30-day video + audio)  
**Power:** Wired only (16–24V AC) — no battery  
**Price:** ~$180  
**Best For:** Two-party states (CA/FL/IL), full-duplex conversation, Google homes

### 2. Best Half-Duplex Doorbell: Ring Battery Doorbell Pro (2024) / Ring Stick Up Cam Pro

**Audio:** Half-duplex PTT, 1.5W speaker, dual-mic beamforming  
**Noise Cancel:** "Audio+" DSP (spectral gate + basic DNN) — decent wind rejection  
**Consent Features:** "Audio Recording" toggle per cam; "Motion Verification" reduces false audio triggers  
**Integration:** Ring app, Alexa ("Alexa, talk to front door"), Ring Protect ($4–20/mo)  
**Power:** Battery (Quick Release) + Solar accessory + Hardwire option  
**Price:** ~$180–230  
**Best For:** Battery flexibility, Alexa homes, one-party states

### 3. Best Local/No-Cloud: Reolink Video Doorbell PoE / Reolink Argus 4 Pro

**Audio:** Half-duplex, 1W speaker, single MEMS mic (PoE) / dual-mic (Argus 4 Pro)  
**Noise Cancel:** Basic spectral subtraction — acceptable in quiet suburbs  
**Consent Features:** **No cloud** — audio recorded to NVR/microSD only; You control retention  
**Integration:** Reolink App, ONVIF, NVR, Home Assistant (via RTSP/ONVIF), no HomeKit  
**Power:** PoE (doorbell) / Battery+Solar (Argus 4 Pro)  
**Price:** ~$130 (PoE) / ~$220 (Argus 4 Pro + solar)  
**Best For:** Privacy absolutists, NVR owners, no-subscription

### 4. Best Indoor Full-Duplex: Google Nest Cam (Wired, 2nd Gen) / Nest Cam IQ (discontinued but available)

**Audio:** Full-duplex, 3-mic array, 2W speaker, Google TPU DNN noise suppression  
**Use Case:** Baby monitor, elderly check-in, pet talk — **phone-call quality**  
**Privacy:** Hardware LED indicator when mic active; Audio recording off by default  
**Price:** ~$100  
**Best For:** Indoor two-way conversation, Google Home

### 5. Best Business Intercom: UniFi G4 Doorbell Pro / 2N IP Verso / Aiphone IX

**Audio:** Full-duplex, 3W+ speaker, acoustic echo cancellation, SIP/VoIP integration  
**Features:** Door release relay, keypad/RFID, elevator control, elevator audio, multi-tenant  
**Compliance:** 2N/Aiphone built for commercial code (ADA, fire panel integration)  
**Price:** G4 Doorbell Pro ~$300; 2N IP Verso ~$800+; Aiphone IX ~$1,200+  
**Best For:** Offices, apartment buildings, gated communities, access control integration

### 6. Best Budget Half-Duplex: Wyze Cam v4 / Blink Outdoor 4 / TP-Link Tapo C120

**Audio:** PTT, 0.5–1W speaker, single mic, basic noise gate  
**Quality:** "Walkie-talkie at best" — fine for "package at door," not for conversation  
**Cloud:** Required for audio (Wyze/Blink); Tapo supports local microSD  
**Price:** $30–60  
**Best For:** Secondary cams, one-party states, low-stakes communication

## Audio Quality Test Checklist (Before Mounting)

1. **Download app, pair camera**
2. **Stand 10 ft away**, speak normally → have partner listen on phone
3. **Stand 3 ft away**, speak → check for clipping/distortion
4. **Play music near camera** (60 dB) → speak → check noise suppression
5. **Simulate wind** (fan on low near mic) → speak → check wind rejection
6. **Full-duplex test:** Both talk simultaneously → check echo/feedback
7. **Latency check:** Count "1-2-3" → measure delay to phone (target &lt;200 ms half, &lt;100 ms full)
8. **Battery cam:** Talk for 60s → check battery drop (should be &lt;2% for half-duplex)

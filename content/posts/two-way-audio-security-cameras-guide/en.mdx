Two-way audio turns a camera into an intercom: talk to delivery drivers, deter intruders, check on kids/pets, or communicate with employees. But not all "two-way audio" is equal — half-duplex (walkie-talkie) vs full-duplex (phone call), noise cancellation quality, and legal consent requirements vary wildly.

<Badge variant="outline">Key Distinction</Badge> **Half-duplex** = push-to-talk, one direction at a time (most budget cams). **Full-duplex** = simultaneous talk/listen like a phone (premium cams, dedicated intercoms).

## Audio Architecture: What's Inside

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="mic">
    <AccordionTrigger>Microphone Types & Placement</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems)** — Standard in modern cams. Tiny, durable, flat frequency response.  
- **Omnidirectional** — Picks up 360°; good for room coverage, bad for focused pickup  
- **Directional (beamforming array)** — 2–4 MEMS + DSP steering; focuses on voice, rejects background  

**Placement matters:**
- Doorbell: Bottom face, angled down → captures visitor at 3–5 ft
- Turret/Dome: Under lens or side → captures 10–20 ft radius
- Bullet: Rear/side vent → often muffled by housing
- Indoor: Top front → best for room conversation

**Wind/weather:** Look for **acoustic mesh** (Gore-Tex / ePTFE) over mic port — blocks water, passes sound. Cheap cams skip this → rain = static.
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="speaker">
    <AccordionTrigger>Speaker Output & Amplification</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**Typical specs:** 0.5–2W Class D amp + 28–40mm driver → 80–100 dB SPL @ 1 ft  
**Real-world:** 85 dB = comfortable conversation at 6 ft; 95 dB = audible across 30 ft yard

**Power supply matters:**
- PoE (802.3af 15.4W) → plenty for 2W audio + camera
- 12V DC → fine
- Battery cams → **audio kills battery** (amp draws 200–500 mA). Ring/Eufy limit talk time to 30–60s on battery.

**Distortion:** Cheap Class D amps clip at max volume. Look for **THD <1% @ 1W** or "HD Audio" marketing (usually means better amp + DSP).
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="duplex">
    <AccordionTrigger>Half-Duplex vs Full-Duplex: The Experience Gap</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
| Mode | How It Works | Latency | Best For | Cameras |
|------|--------------|---------|----------|---------|
| **Half-Duplex (PTT)** | Press button → talk → release → listen | 200–500 ms | Doorbell, gate, quick "leave package" | Ring, Arlo, Blink, Wyze, Reolink (most), Eufy (most) |
| **Full-Duplex** | Simultaneous TX/RX; echo cancellation | 50–150 ms | Baby monitor, elderly care, business intercom | Nest Doorbell (wired), Google Nest Cam, Arlo Ultra 2, UniFi G4 Doorbell Pro, Axis, 2N, Aiphone |

**Echo Cancellation (AEC) is the differentiator:**  
- Half-duplex doesn't need AEC (mic muted while speaker plays)  
- Full-duplex **requires** adaptive AEC (NLMS/RLS/Kalman) — without it, you hear your own voice delayed = unusable

**Test before buying:** Call the camera from app, speak while it's playing. If you hear echo/feedback → AEC fails.
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

## Noise Cancellation: What Actually Works

<Callout type="note">
**DSP Chain in Good Cameras:** Beamforming → Stationary Noise Suppression (Wiener/Kalman) → Non-Stationary Suppression (DNN) → AEC (full-duplex) → AGC (auto gain) → Limiter
</Callout>

| Noise Type | Suppression Method | Typical Reduction |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Wind | Physical mesh + spectral gating | 15–25 dB |
| HVAC / Fan / Traffic | Spectral subtraction / Wiener | 10–20 dB |
| Dog bark / Kid scream | DNN-based non-stationary (RNNoise, NSNet2) | 15–30 dB |
| Your own voice (echo) | Adaptive AEC (NLMS/Kalman) | 25–40 dB |
| Speaker feedback (howl) | Notch filtering + gain margin | Prevention |

**Marketing vs Reality:** "AI Noise Cancellation" = usually just spectral gate. **Real DNN noise suppression** needs NPU (Ambarella CV25, Rockchip RV1126, Google TPU, Apple Neural Engine). Check specs: "RNNoise", "NSNet2", "DNN-based", "Deep learning noise reduction."

## Legal: One-Party vs Two-Party Consent

<Callout type="warning">
**Audio recording laws are stricter than video.** Video = reasonable expectation of privacy (varies). Audio = wiretap statutes in many states. **Consent requirements apply to *recording*, not live listen.**
</Callout>

### US State Consent Laws (2026)

| Consent Type | States | What It Means |
|--------------|--------|---------------|
| **One-Party** (38 states + DC) | AL, AK, AZ, AR, CO, DE, DC, GA, HI, ID, IN, IA, KS, KY, LA, ME, MN, MS, MO, NE, NV, NJ, NM, NY, NC, ND, OH, OK, RI, SC, SD, TN, TX, UT, VT, VA, WV, WI, WY | You can record if *you* are a party to conversation. No notice needed. |
| **Two-Party / All-Party** (12 states) | CA, CT, FL, IL, MD, MA, MI, MT, NH, OR, PA, WA | **All parties must consent.** Signage + verbal notice required. |
| **Hybrid / Complex** | VT (one-party but strict), OR (all-party for in-person) | Check local counsel |

**Federal (Wiretap Act):** One-party consent baseline. State law can be stricter.

### Practical Compliance for Camera Owners

  <AccordionItem value="residential">
    <AccordionTrigger>Residential (Doorbell, Yard, Interior)</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
**One-party state:** You're a party → record freely. Still: **post signage** "Audio recording in use" — deters disputes, helps evidence admissibility.

**Two-party state:** 
- **Doorbell visitor:** Not a party until they speak. **Must notify** before recording.
- **Solution:** Enable "Audio Recording: Off" by default; only record video. Or use Ring/Arlo "Privacy Zones" for audio (some support).
- **Signage:** "Audio and video recording in progress — by entering you consent" at gate/door.
- **Best practice:** Disable audio recording entirely in CA/FL/IL/MA/MD/PA/WA unless you have explicit consent workflow.
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="business">
    <AccordionTrigger>Business / Commercial</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
- **Employees:** Written consent in handbook/onboarding (all states)
- **Customers:** Signage at entrance + POS ("Audio recording for security")
- **Break rooms / Restrooms:** **Never record audio** (high privacy expectation = illegal in most states)
- **Call centers / Phones:** Separate federal/state telephony recording laws (often all-party)
- **Retention:** 30 days max unless litigation hold; auto-delete policy documented
    </AccordionContent>
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="landlord">
    <AccordionTrigger>Landlord / Multi-Family</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
- **Common areas (lobby, hallway):** Video OK; Audio = **high risk**. Tenants not parties to each other's conversations.
- **Unit interiors:** **Never** — tenant has reasonable expectation of privacy.
- **Doorbell cams on tenant doors:** Tenant owns the cam → tenant's consent applies. Landlord cannot access audio without tenant consent.
- **Lease addendum:** "Tenant may install video doorbell; audio recording subject to state consent laws; landlord not liable."

## Audio Quality Test Checklist (Before Mounting)

1. **Download app, pair camera**
2. **Stand 10 ft away**, speak normally → have partner listen on phone
3. **Stand 3 ft away**, speak → check for clipping/distortion
4. **Play music near camera** (60 dB) → speak → check noise suppression
5. **Simulate wind** (fan on low near mic) → speak → check wind rejection
6. **Full-duplex test:** Both talk simultaneously → check echo/feedback
7. **Latency check:** Count "1-2-3" → measure delay to phone (target <200 ms half, <100 ms full)
8. **Battery cam:** Talk for 60s → check battery drop (should be <2% for half-duplex)

When you are choosing cameras, the three resolutions you will actually see are **1080p (2MP)**, **5MP**, and **4K (8MP)**. Each tier serves a different job, and the right answer is almost never "the highest one everywhere." This guide compares all three so you can assign the correct resolution to each camera location.

## The quick answer

| Tier        | Pixels (approx) | Face ID distance | Relative storage | Best for                         |
| ----------- | --------------- | ---------------- | ---------------- | -------------------------------- |
| 1080p (2MP) | 1920 × 1080     | 15-25 ft         | 1×               | Close indoor coverage            |
| 5MP         | ~2880 × 1620    | 25-45 ft         | ~1.6×            | General coverage (sweet spot)    |
| 4K (8MP)    | 3840 × 2160     | 50-80 ft         | ~2.5×            | Wide/high-risk areas, long range |

**5MP is the balanced default** for most coverage; step down to 1080p indoors and up to 4K only where long-range identification matters.

## Detail and identification distance

Under DORI standards with a typical 4mm lens:

- **1080p:** identify a face at ~15-25 ft.
- **5MP:** identify a face at ~25-45 ft.
- **4K:** identify a face at ~50-80 ft.

So 4K is not "a bit sharper" — it roughly doubles 5MP's useful range. That is why 4K earns its keep at entrances, gates, and parking approaches, while 1080p is wasted on a wide driveway.

## Low-light performance

Night quality follows sensor size and pixel size, not megapixels. On a small sensor, 1080p and 5MP often beat 4K after dark. Practical rules:

- Dim indoor area → a **Starlight 1080p** or **Starlight 5MP** camera.
- Lit outdoor area → **5MP** is the sweet spot.
- Large, bright outdoor area needing long-range ID → **4K** with at least a 1/1.8" sensor (look for Starlight/ColorVu).

## Storage and bandwidth

Relative daily storage (H.265, continuous):

- 1080p: ~10-15 GB/day
- 5MP: ~16-25 GB/day
- 4K: ~30-50 GB/day

4K is about 2.5x the data of 1080p. Use **H.265+** compression and **motion-only recording** (cuts 60-70%) to keep NVR size sane, especially in all-4K deployments.

## Mixing tiers by zone (recommended)

A modern NVR mixes resolutions without issue. A sensible plan:

- **Entry, gate, perimeter:** 4K for identification-grade footage.
- **General coverage (rooms, floors, yards):** 5MP — the best detail-to-cost ratio.
- **Constrained indoor spaces (hallways, small rooms):** 1080p to save storage.

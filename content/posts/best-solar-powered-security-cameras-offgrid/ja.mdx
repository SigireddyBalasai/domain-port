Solar security cameras promise "install once, run forever." Reality: undersized panels, lithium-ion cold-weather failure, and cloud-dependent recording turn off-grid into off-line by February. This guide separates marketing from physics—panel watts, battery chemistry, duty cycles, and cellular fallback for sites beyond Wi-Fi.

<Badge variant="outline">Physics First</Badge> **Solar harvest = Panel Watts ×
Peak Sun Hours × 0.75 (system losses).** A 5W panel in Arizona (6.5 hrs) = 24
Wh/day. In Maine winter (1.5 hrs) = 5.6 Wh/day. Size for your *worst* month, not
your best.

## The Solar Camera Equation

<Callout type="note">

<strong>Daily Energy Budget:</strong> Camera average draw (W) × 24 hrs = Wh/day
needed. Panel must produce 1.3–2× that (winter buffer + battery charge
efficiency). Battery must hold 3–7 days autonomy (no sun).

</Callout>

| Camera Type                                           | Avg Draw  | Daily Need | Min Panel (Sunbelt) | Min Panel (North/ Winter) | Battery Target    |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | --------- | ---------- | ------------------- | ------------------------- | ----------------- |
| **PIR Wake + Clip** (Eufy, Ring, Arlo)                | 0.3–0.5 W | 7–12 Wh    | 3–5 W               | 10–15 W                   | 5,000–10,000 mAh  |
| **Continuous + AI** (Reolink Argus 4 Pro, Go PT Plus) | 1.5–3 W   | 36–72 Wh   | 15–25 W             | 50–80 W                   | 15,000–30,000 mAh |
| **PTZ + 4G LTE** (Reolink Go PT Plus, Vosker V300)    | 2–4 W     | 48–96 Wh   | 25–40 W             | 80–150 W                  | 20,000–40,000 mAh |

**Rule:** If spec sheet doesn't list _average power consumption_, assume 3× the "standby" claim.

<Callout type="warning">

<strong>Winter Reality:</strong> Li-ion cameras (Eufy, Ring, Arlo, Argus 3/4)
**stop charging below 32°F**. They run on battery until it dies, then brick
until warmed. LiFePO₄ or heated enclosures are mandatory for Zone 5 and colder.

</Callout>

## Solar Panel Sizing Calculator

<Callout type="info">

<strong>Quick Formula:</strong> `(Camera Avg Watts × 24) ÷ (Peak Sun Hours ×
0.75) = Min Panel Watts`

</Callout>

| Location      | Peak Sun Hours (Annual Avg) | Peak Sun Hours (Dec Avg) | Panel Multiplier (Winter) |
| ------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------- |
| Phoenix, AZ   | 6.5                         | 3.5                      | 2.0×                      |
| Dallas, TX    | 5.3                         | 2.8                      | 2.5×                      |
| Atlanta, GA   | 5.0                         | 2.5                      | 2.8×                      |
| Denver, CO    | 5.5                         | 2.2                      | 3.2×                      |
| Chicago, IL   | 4.3                         | 1.5                      | 4.5×                      |
| Boston, MA    | 4.1                         | 1.3                      | 5.2×                      |
| Seattle, WA   | 3.9                         | 1.0                      | 6.5×                      |
| Fairbanks, AK | 3.3                         | 0.1                      | 70× (don't bother)        |

**Example:** Reolink Go PT Plus (2.5W avg) in Denver winter (2.2 hrs):  
`(2.5 × 24) ÷ (2.2 × 0.75) = 36.4 W minimum panel` → **Use 50W+ panel with MPPT**

## Mounting & Wiring Best Practices

<Accordion type="single" collapsible>
  <AccordionItem value="mount">
    <AccordionTrigger>Panel Orientation & Tilt</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>

<strong>Fixed mount:</strong> Face true south (azimuth 180°), tilt = latitude +
15° for winter optimization
<strong>Example:</strong> Denver (40° lat) → 55° tilt
<strong>Adjustable seasonal:</strong> Latitude -15° (summer), Latitude
(spring/fall), Latitude +15° (winter)
<strong>RV portable:</strong> Aim manually 2–3× daily; use Sun Surveyor /
SolarWatch app for real-time azimuth
<strong>No shade:</strong> Even 10% shading cuts output 50%+ (bypass diodes help
but not magic)

</AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="wire">
    <AccordionTrigger>Wire Gauge & Voltage Drop</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>

<strong>12V systems are current-heavy.</strong> 5A at 12V = 60W but 5A drops
voltage fast.

<ul>
  <li>Panel → Controller: 10 AWG (up to 20 ft), 8 AWG (20–40 ft)</li>
  <li>Controller → Battery: 10 AWG (short as possible)</li>
  <li>
    Battery → Camera: 18–22 AWG (camera pigtail) — keep run &lt;6 ft; use 12V
    distribution block at battery
  </li>
</ul>
<strong>Fuse everything:</strong> 15A fuse at battery +, 5A at camera +.
Marine-grade fuse blocks (Blue Sea Systems).

</AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem value="cold">
    <AccordionTrigger>Cold Weather Battery Protection</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>

<strong>Li-ion cameras (Eufy, Ring, Arlo, Argus 3/4):</strong>
<ul>
  <li>
    Build insulated box (1" polyiso + reflective bubble) around camera body —
    keeps battery 15–20°F warmer
  </li>
  <li>
    Add 5W 12V silicone heater pad (thermostat 40°F) on battery pack — runs off
    solar controller load output
  </li>
  <li>Or: Pull camera inside weekly to warm-charge (painful but works)</li>
</ul>

<strong>LiFePO₄ (Go PT Plus, Vosker, custom):</strong>
<ul>
  <li>
    Charge down to -10°F with BMS heater (built into Reolink LiFePO₄ pack)
  </li>
  <li>Still insulate — capacity drops 20% at 0°F but recovers</li>
  <li>No heater needed above -10°F</li>
</ul>

</AccordionContent>

  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

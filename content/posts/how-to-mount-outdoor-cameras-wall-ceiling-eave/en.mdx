The camera is only as good as its mount. A $400 camera on two drywall screws will fail. This guide covers every exterior surface with the right hardware, drill bits, sealants, and structural principles so your install lasts 10+ years.

<Badge variant="outline">Golden Rule</Badge> **Anchor to structure, not skin.**
Screws into vinyl, stucco, or thin soffit without hitting a stud/rafter = future
failure. Find the wood/metal behind the cladding.

## Surface-Specific Mounting

### 1. Wood Siding / Trim / Fascia (Easiest — Direct to Stud)

**Tools:** Stud finder, ⅛" drill bit, #10 × 2.5" coated deck screws, level

    <AccordionTrigger>Step-by-Step: Wood Surface</AccordionTrigger>
    <AccordionContent>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Locate studs</strong> — Electronic stud finder (deep scan) or knock + confirm with small finish nail</li>
  <li><strong>Mark camera bracket holes</strong> — Level bracket, mark through holes</li>
  <li><strong>Pilot drill</strong> — ⅛" bit at each mark (prevents splitting)</li>
  <li><strong>Mount bracket</strong> — #10 × 2.5" coated deck screws, 2 per hole minimum</li>
  <li><strong>Seal</strong> — Silicone around screw heads if bracket doesn't have gasket</li>
  <li><strong>Attach camera</strong> — Verify level again before final tighten</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Pro Tip:</strong> Fascia board (vertical face under roof edge) is often 2× material — <strong>ideal for eave mounts</strong>. Screw directly into it with 3" lag bolts.</p>

### 2. Vinyl / Aluminum Siding (Use a Mounting Block)

<Callout type="warning">

**Never** drive screws through vinyl siding into sheathing only. Wind load +
camera weight = ripped-out siding in first storm.

</Callout>

**Solution: Vinyl Siding Mounting Block (J-Channel Compatible)**

| Brand         | Model           | Fits                   | Price |
| ------------- | --------------- | ---------------------- | ----- |
| **EZ Siding** | Universal Block | All J-channel          | $12   |
| **Ply Gem**   | Mounting Block  | Ply Gem / CertainTeed  | $15   |
| **Generic**   | 2-Piece Block   | Universal (cut to fit) | $8    |

**Install:**

1. **Remove siding panel** (unlock J-channel with zip tool)
2. **Position block** over sheathing, centered on stud
3. **Screw block to sheathing/stud** — #8 × 1.5" screws (4 corners)
4. **Re-install siding** — snaps over block flange
5. **Mount camera bracket** to block — #8 × 1" screws

**Alternative (no block):** **3M VHB 5952 Tape** + **1 screw to stud** (hybrid). Clean with IPA, press 30 sec, add one structural screw.

### 3. Stucco / Brick / Concrete / Masonry

**Tools:** **Hammer drill** (SDS+ rotary hammer best), masonry bits, Tapcon/sleeve anchors, shop vac

| ------------------------- | ---------- | --------- | ----------------------------- | ------------ |
| **Tapcon ¼" × 2¼"**       | ³/₁₆"      | 1"        | Brick, block, concrete        | 200+ (shear) |
| **Sleeve Anchor ⅜" × 3"** | ⅜"         | 1.5"      | Heavy cams, brick             | 500+         |

| **Plastic Anchor + Lag**  | ¼"         | 1.5"      | Light cams, stucco over frame | 50           |
<p><strong>Install Steps:</strong></p>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Mark holes</strong> — level bracket, mark through holes</li>
  <li><strong>Drill</strong> — Hammer drill, correct bit, <strong>depth = anchor length + ¼"</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Blow out dust</strong> — Shop vac + compressed air (critical for holding power)</li>
  <li><strong>Tap anchor</strong> — Hammer until flush (Tapcon: drive with impact driver)</li>
  <li><strong>Mount bracket</strong> — Washer + nut (sleeve/wedge) or screw (Tapcon)</li>
  <li><strong>Seal</strong> — Silicone around each anchor penetration</li>
</ol>

**Stucco Specific:** Drill through stucco into **wood sheathing/stud** if possible (longer Tapcon 3"). If solid masonry behind, use sleeve anchor. **Never** rely on stucco alone — it crumbles.

### 4. Hardie Board / Fiber Cement / LP SmartSide

- **Pre-drill** ⅛" pilot at every screw (prevents cracking)
- **Screw to stud** — #10 × 2.5" coated, 16" OC max
- **Caulk heads** — Paintable acrylic latex (matches siding)
- **Avoid** driving near edges (1" minimum from edge)

### 5. Soffit / Eave Mounts (Best for Overhead Coverage)

**Soffit Mount (Horizontal Surface):**

- **Bracket:** Right-angle bracket with **base plate** (4" × 4" min)
- **Fastener:** **Lag bolt ⅜" × 3"** into **rafter tail** (not soffit panel!)
- **Find rafter:** Drill ¼" exploratory hole through soffit → feel for wood
- **Seal:** Butyl tape under bracket base + silicone around bolts

**Eave / Fascia Mount (Vertical Face):**

- **Direct to fascia board** (usually 2×) — #10 × 3" deck screws or ⅜" lag bolts
- **Or:** Rafter tail through soffit (see above)
- **Angle bracket** if camera needs tilt down

<Callout type="tip">

**Eave mount = best weather protection + optimal angle.** Camera looks down
20–30°, covered by roof overhang. Use right-angle bracket + 3" lag into rafter
tail.

</Callout>

### 6. Pole Mount (Driveway, Gate, Perimeter)

**Pole Spec:** 1.5" or 2" Schedule 40 steel pipe (not fence top rail)

**Footing:**

- **Depth:** 18–24" (frost line + 6")
- **Diameter:** 8–10" (Sonotube or direct pour)
- **Concrete:** 5000 psi mix, 2–3 bags per hole
- **Rebar:** 2× #4 vertical, tied
- **Conduit:** ¾" PVC sweep up inside pole for cable

**Camera Bracket:** **U-bolt pole mount** (adjustable tilt/pan)

- Stainless U-bolts + nylon lock nuts
- Level bracket before final tighten

**Grounding (NEC 250.52):**

- #6 CU solid from pole base to **ground rod** (8' copper-clad)
- Bond camera bracket to pole with #10 CU jumper

### 7. Tree / Wooden Post Mount

- **Stainless steel strap** (Band-It, ¾" × 0.030") + **buckle**
- **Or:** ⅜" × 4" lag shield + lag bolt into post heartwood
- **Allow movement:** Camera on **articulating arm** (not rigid) — trees sway
- **Inspect annually** — tighten strap, check lag

## Cable Entry & Weatherproofing

| Method                      | Best For                  | Install                              |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Cable Gland (IP68)**      | Pole, box, bracket        | Drill ⅞" hole, insert gland, tighten |
| **Conduit Body (LB/LL/LR)** | Wall transitions          | PVC or metal, sealed with duct seal  |
| **Silicone + Grommet**      | Quick, low-profile        | ⅞" hole, silicone bead, push cable   |
| **Junction Box + Cover**    | Multiple cables, code req | 4" round box, gasketed cover         |

**Drip Loop:** **Always** form 6" loop **below** entry point — water runs down cable, drips off loop, not into hole.

## Structural Engineering Basics (Don't Skip)

**Wind Load:** 4 lb camera + 6" arm @ 90 mph = **~35 lb moment** at fastener.  
**Leverage:** Every inch of bracket extension multiplies force.

**Fastener Spacing Rules:**

- **Lag bolts:** 3" min embed, 2 per bracket minimum
- **Tapcons:** 4" OC max, 1.5" edge distance
- **Screws to studs:** 2 per bracket leg, 16" OC if multiple brackets

**Pull-Out Values (lb) — Typical:**
| Fastener | Into Stud | Into Concrete |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| #10 × 2.5" screw | 250 | — |
| ⅜" × 3" Lag bolt | 400 | — |
| ¼" Tapcon × 2.25" | — | 300 |
| ⅜" Sleeve anchor | — | 600 |
| ½" Wedge anchor | — | 1200 |

**Design for 4× safety factor.** If camera + bracket = 5 lb, mount must hold 20+ lb per fastener.

## Code & Permit Checklist

- [ ] **NEC 300.4** — Cable protection (1.25" from edge, conduit if &lt;1.25")
- [ ] **NEC 410.10** — Luminaire (camera) support — listed for weight
- [ ] **NEC 250** — Grounding metal poles, boxes, conduits
- [ ] **IRC R302** — Fireblocking at penetrations (caulk or mineral wool)
- [ ] **Local permit** — Many jurisdictions: >$500 or >6 ft height = permit
- [ ] **HOA approval** — Submit photos + specs before drill

Running cable is 70% of PoE camera installation labor. Do it right once: solid copper, proper pathways, correct termination, verified testing. This guide covers every step from spool to switch.

<Badge variant="outline">Cable Choice</Badge> **Use solid copper Cat6
(CMR/CMP).** Not CCA (copper-clad aluminum). Not stranded. Not Cat5e for new
runs. Solid copper Cat6 = 100m at 1Gbps PoE+ without voltage drop issues.

---

## Cable Selection: What to Buy (And Avoid)

| Cable Type                  | Conductor       | PoE+ (30W) Max | PoE++ (60/90W) Max | Rating             | Use Case            |
| --------------------------- | --------------- | -------------- | ------------------ | ------------------ | ------------------- |
| **Cat6 Solid Copper (CMR)** | 23 AWG Solid Cu | 100 m (328 ft) | 80 m (262 ft)      | Riser (in-wall)    | **Standard choice** |
| **Cat6 Solid Copper (CMP)** | 23 AWG Solid Cu | 100 m          | 80 m               | Plenum (air ducts) | Required in plenums |
| **Cat6a Solid Copper**      | 23 AWG Solid Cu | 100 m          | 100 m              | Riser/Plenum       | Future-proof 10G    |
| **Outdoor Direct Burial**   | 23 AWG Solid Cu | 100 m          | 80 m               | Gel-filled, UV     | Underground, aerial |
| **❌ Cat5e**                | 24 AWG          | 100 m          | 50 m               | Riser              | Legacy only         |
| **❌ CCA (Copper-Clad Al)** | Aluminum core   | **FAIL**       | **FAIL**           | Any                | **Never for PoE**   |
| **❌ Stranded / Patch**     | Stranded Cu     | 10 m (33 ft)   | 5 m                | Patch              | Patch cords only    |

<Callout type="danger">

**CCA (Copper-Clad Aluminum) fails at PoE.** Aluminum has 60% higher
resistance → voltage drop → camera reboots at night (IR on). **Verify "Solid
Bare Copper" on jacket print.**

</Callout>

### Basement/Crawl to Exterior Wall

- **Drill through rim joist** (not foundation) — ⅞" hole, 12" above grade minimum
- **Fireblock:** Stuff mineral wool + fire caulk around cable at penetration
- **Exterior:** LB conduit body → ½" LFMC to camera box

### Interior Wall to Exterior (Retrofit)

1. **Cut old-work box hole** at camera height (inside)
2. **Drill down** through bottom plate into basement/crawl (⅞" auger)
3. **Fish up** from basement with glow rods
4. **Protect:** Nail plates on studs where cable passes (NEC 300.4)

## Termination: RJ45 Pass-Through (Fastest, Most Reliable)

<Callout type="note">

**Use pass-through connectors.** Standard RJ45 requires precise trim.
Pass-through lets you verify color order _before_ crimp. **Tool:** Ratcheting
pass-through crimper (Klein VDV226-110, $45).

</Callout>

### T568B Pinout (Standard for PoE)

| Pin | Pair | Color        | PoE Function   |
| --- | ---- | ------------ | -------------- |
| 1   | 2    | Orange/White | Data + PoE (+) |
| 2   | 2    | Orange       | Data + PoE (+) |
| 3   | 3    | Green/White  | Data + PoE (-) |
| 4   | 1    | Blue         | PoE (+) Spare  |
| 5   | 1    | Blue/White   | PoE (+) Spare  |
| 6   | 3    | Green        | Data + PoE (-) |
| 7   | 4    | Brown/White  | PoE (-) Spare  |
| 8   | 4    | Brown        | PoE (-) Spare  |

**PoE uses ALL 4 pairs** (802.3bt). **All 8 conductors must be perfect.**

### Crimp Procedure (Pass-Through)

1. **Strip 1.5"** jacket — don't nick conductors
2. **Untwist pairs** only as needed — keep twists close to connector
3. **Arrange T568B** — fan flat, verify order left-to-right
4. **Insert fully** — conductors visible at front face
5. **Verify order** — look through clear connector
6. **Crimp** — ratchet tool, one firm squeeze
7. **Tug test** — 5 lb pull, no movement

## Keystone Jacks (Wall Plate Termination)

**When:** Camera connects to patch panel / wall jack → patch cable to camera

**Punch-Down (110/Keystone):**

1. Strip 2" jacket
2. Seat wires in color-coded slots (T568B printed on jack)
3. **Punch straight down** — 110 tool, one strike per wire
4. Trim excess flush
5. Snap into wall plate

**Test:** Continuity + PoE voltage at jack before camera connects.

## Testing: Verify Every Run

<Callout type="warning">

**Never trust a cable untested.** 30% of "camera failures" are cable faults.
Test _before_ mounting camera.

</Callout>

### Tier 1: Continuity + Wiremap (Every Cable)

**Tool:** Basic tester (NF-8601, $25) — 8 LEDs + shield

- **All 8 green** = pass
- **Any red/open/cross** = re-terminate

### Tier 2: PoE Voltage Under Load (Critical)

**Tool:** PoE Tester (LINKSYS PTPoE, $35) or Multimeter + PoE Injector

1. Connect injector → cable → tester (or camera)
2. Measure **at camera end**:

- **PoE (802.3af):** 44–57V DC
- **PoE+ (802.3at):** 50–57V DC
- **PoE++ (802.3bt):** 52–57V DC

3. **Under load** (camera powered on) — voltage drop &lt;3V from injector

### Tier 3: Data Rate Validation (Pro)

**Tool:** Cable Certifier (Fluke DSX-5000, $8k) — rental $150/day

- **Cat6:** 250 MHz, 1 Gbps guaranteed
- **Cat6a:** 500 MHz, 10 Gbps guaranteed
- **Print report** for warranty

## PoE Voltage Drop Calculator

**Formula:** `Vdrop = (2 × Length_ft × Current_A × Resistance_Ω_per_1000ft) / 1000`

| Cable              | Resistance (Ω/1000ft) | Max 30W (0.6A) | Max 60W (1.2A) | Max 90W (1.8A) |
| ------------------ | --------------------- | -------------- | -------------- | -------------- |
| **Cat6 (23 AWG)**  | 6.6                   | **328 ft**     | **164 ft**     | **109 ft**     |
| **Cat6a (23 AWG)** | 6.6                   | 328 ft         | 328 ft         | 218 ft         |
| **Cat5e (24 AWG)** | 8.5                   | 250 ft         | 125 ft         | 83 ft          |

**Rule:** Stay under **80% of max** for margin. For PoE++ (60/90W), use Cat6a or limit to 100 ft on Cat6.

## Common Faults & Fixes

| Symptom                             | Cause                                   | Fix                                            |
| ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Camera reboots at night (IR on)** | Voltage drop >3V                        | Shorten run / Cat6a / PoE++ injector at camera |
| **Link flaps 100/1000 Mbps**        | Single pair open / high resistance      | Re-terminate both ends; test each pair         |
| **No link, tester shows 7/8 wires** | One conductor broken / loose            | Re-crimp; check for staple crush               |
| **Crossed pairs (1↔3, 2↔6)**        | T568A one end, T568B other              | Standardize T568B both ends                    |
| **Water in cable (corrosion)**      | Outdoor cable not gel-filled / bad seal | Replace with direct burial; seal entries       |
| **EMI noise (video artifacts)**     | Parallel to Romex / HVAC                | Separate 12"+ ; cross at 90° ; shielded Cat6   |

## Pro Tips from 100+ Installs

1. **Label both ends** before pull — Sharpie + cable label (Brother PT-E110)
2. **Pull string first** — Leave mason line for future runs
3. **Service loops** — 3 ft at camera, 6 ft at NVR/switch
4. **Color code** — Different jacket colors per zone (Blue=Front, Green=Back, Yellow=Side)
5. **Document** — Photo of each termination, cable schedule PDF, as-built diagram
6. **Test before mount** — Camera on ladder = 10× harder to fix
7. **PoE budget** — Switch total PoE budget ≥ Σ camera max draw × 1.2
8. **Surge protection** — Outdoor runs: PoE surge protector (Transtector, $30) at building entry

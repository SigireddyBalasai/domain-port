- **Resolution**: 4K UltraHD at $130 (Amcrest), 4MP at $60 (Reolink), or 2K at $80 (TP-Link)

- **Night Vision**: Look for Starlight sensors at this price point — they outperform standard IR in low light
- **Smart Detection**: Person/vehicle detection is now standard at $60+
- **Weatherproof Rating**: IP66 minimum for outdoor installation

## In-Depth Reviews

### Reolink RLC-410 — Best Budget PoE Pick

At $59.99, the Reolink RLC-410 is the most affordable way to get dependable wired surveillance. Its 4MP sensor delivers crisp 2560x1440 footage, and the built-in Person/Vehicle Detection cuts down on false alerts from trees or passing cars. Because it runs on PoE, you only need one Ethernet cable for both power and data — ideal if you are already pulling cable to a garage, backyard, or entrance.

**Where it shines:** Easy PoE install, reliable night vision out to 30m, strong value.
**Watch out for:** No onboard storage — pair it with an NVR or a PoE camera that records to microSD. You will also need a PoE injector or switch.

### TP-Link Tapo C310 — Best WiFi Option

The Tapo C310 brings 2K QHD video at $79.99 without locking you into a subscription for basic features. Setup is handled entirely through the Tapo app, and it slots neatly into a smart-home routine with Alexa and Google Assistant support. It is the right call when running Ethernet cable is not practical.

**Where it shines:** Sharp 2K image, simple app setup, good 30m night vision, no mandatory subscription.
**Watch out for:** WiFi-only (needs a solid 2.4GHz signal), and the microSD card is sold separately.

### Amcrest UltraHD 4K — Best for Detail

If you need to read a license plate or recognize a face across a driveway, the Amcrest UltraHD 4K at $129.99 is the pick. Its Starlight sensor pulls in usable color detail in low light where standard IR cameras would be monochrome, and the 112° wide angle covers more ground per camera.

**Where it shines:** True 4K clarity, excellent low-light Starlight performance, wide field of view.
**Watch out for:** Higher price, and some advanced features benefit from PoE++ power.

## How to Choose the Right Camera

Start with your installation constraint. If you can run a cable, PoE wins on reliability — choose the Reolink for budget or the Amcrest for detail. If cabling is impossible, the Tapo C310 keeps you subscription-free on WiFi. Then match resolution to distance: 4MP or 2K covers most homes, while 4K earns its keep for long driveways and entry points where detail matters.
